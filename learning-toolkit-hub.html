What Is Metacognition?

Definition: Metacognition is "thinking about thinking" - your awareness and understanding of your own thought processes.


Key Components:

  • Planning: Setting goals and strategies before learning
  • Monitoring: Checking your understanding while learning
  • Evaluating: Reflecting on what worked after learning

Top 5 Study Myths—Debunked

Myth 1: Highlighting helps you learn better
Reality: Active recall and practice testing are more effective

Myth 2: Cramming works for long-term retention
Reality: Spaced repetition creates lasting memories

Myth 3: You should stick to one learning style
Reality: Multi-modal learning is most effective

Memory Enhancement Strategies

The Science: Research shows these techniques significantly improve retention:


  • Elaborative Rehearsal: Connect new info to existing knowledge
  • The Method of Loci: Use spatial memory for complex information
  • Interleaving: Mix different topics during study sessions
  • Self-Testing: Quiz yourself frequently without looking
