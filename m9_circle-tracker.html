Circle Geometry Mastery Tracker

Solve problems and justify the solution strategy, using the following circle properties:

The perpendicular from the center of a circle to a chord bisects the chord
Example: Draw a radius that intersects a chord and prove the chord is bisected.
The central angle is equal to twice the inscribed angle subtended by the same arc
Example: Given a central and inscribed angle from the same arc, calculate the missing one.
Inscribed angles subtended by the same arc are congruent
Example: Show that angle ABC and angle ADC subtended by the same arc AC are equal.
A tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of tangency
Example: Given a tangent and radius, prove they meet at a right angle.
Can solve problems using one or more circle properties
Example: Use a combination of circle rules to solve for unknown angles or segment lengths in a complex diagram.
