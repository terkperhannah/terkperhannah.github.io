Math 30 Program: CURRENTLY DEVELOPING

In Alberta, Math 30-1 and Math 30-2 are two different pathways within the Grade 12 math curriculum, with 30-1 being more focused on calculus and pre-calculus concepts and 30-2 covering a broader range of mathematical topics, including some calculus but with a stronger emphasis on practical applications and reasoning.

Math 30-1

Functions & Transformations

Explore operations, compositions, and inverses of functions, plus graph transformations including stretches, reflections, and combinations.

Exponential & Logarithmic Functions

Dive into exponential growth and decay, logarithms, laws of logs, and graphing techniques.

Permutations & Combinations

Master counting principles, factorials, restrictions, Pascal’s triangle, and the binomial theorem.

Radical & Rational Functions

Learn to graph and analyze radical and rational functions, including asymptotes and discontinuities.

Trigonometry

Review radians, the unit circle, identities, graphing sinusoidal functions, and solving trig equations.

Diploma Review

Targeted review and practice for your Math 30-1 diploma exam.

Math 30-2

Set Theory

Venn diagrams and set operations

Probability

Conditional probability and counting techniques

Rational Expressions

Simplifying and solving rational equations

Polynomial Functions

Graphing and analyzing polynomial equations

Sinusoidal Functions

Modeling periodic phenomena

Diploma Review

Comprehensive exam preparation
