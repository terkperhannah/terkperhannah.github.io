ISTE Standards for Students

Below are the 7 ISTE Standards for Students, written in student-friendly language with real classroom examples. Click on the images to explore external resources and tools that align with each standard.

1. Empowered Learner

What it means: You take charge of your learning by setting goals, using technology to track progress, and asking for help when needed.

Example: Using Google Classroom or Khan Academy to track your practice quiz scores and decide what to study next.

Image source: Khan Academy, used under fair use for educational purposes

2. Digital Citizen

What it means: You understand how to be safe, respectful, and responsible online.

Example: Citing images you use in school projects and reporting inappropriate content on school platforms.

Image source: Wikimedia Commons. Creative Commons License.

3. Knowledge Constructor

What it means: You use digital tools to find good information, organize it, and make something new from what you learned.

Example: Creating a research presentation using Google Slides after gathering info from trusted science websites.

Image source: National Geographic Education. Educational use image placeholder. Replace with correct attribution if changed.

4. Innovative Designer

What it means: You solve real problems by designing and testing creative solutions.

Example: Using MakeCode to design a Scratch game that teaches players about recycling.

Image source: Microsoft MakeCode (2024). Fair use for educational demonstration.

5. Computational Thinker

What it means: You break down problems into steps and use tech to help you solve them.

Example: Writing code in Replit to program your robot to detect and stop at a red object.

Image source: Replit logo from replit.com. Used under fair use.

6. Creative Communicator

What it means: You use different tools and ways to share your ideas so others can understand and learn from you.

Example: Recording a voice-over slideshow to explain your math project instead of presenting live.

Image source: Canva for Education. Visit canva.com for terms.
