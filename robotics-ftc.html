🏆 FTC Awards Guide - What Judges Look For

Why Awards Matter for Provincials

50% of advancement points come from awards! Teams advance through a combination of field performance (winning matches) AND judged awards. As a rookie team, focusing on awards gives you a real path to provincials even if your robot is still developing.

2025-2026 DECODE Season Award Categories

TA Awards: Connect, Reach, Sustain (only ONE given at smaller events with 4-10 teams)
MCI Awards: Design, Innovate, Control (only ONE given at smaller events with 4-10 teams)

INSPIRE AWARD

TOP AWARD - Best overall team | Advances to Provincials

Role model team that is a strong ambassador for FIRST. Must be a top contender for multiple awards.

  • [REQUIRED] Submit a PORTFOLIO
  • [REQUIRED] Strong contender for MCI Award (Design/Innovate/Control)
  • [REQUIRED] Strong contender for TA Award (Connect/Reach/Sustain)
  • [REQUIRED] Strong contender for Think Award
  • [REQUIRED] Team is positive, inclusive, each member contributes
  • [REQUIRED] Describe, demonstrate, or display experiences to judges

THINK AWARD

Best engineering journey documentation

Reflects the journey the team took throughout their season. Portfolio is the key reference.

  • [REQUIRED] Submit PORTFOLIO with engineering content
  • [REQUIRED] Evidence of engineering process used
  • [REQUIRED] Lessons learned and implemented for robot design
  • [REQUIRED] Trade-off/cost-benefit analysis OR mathematical analysis
  • [Encouraged] How team learns from mentors, skill development plan
  • [Encouraged] How team identified goals and tracked progress

CONNECT AWARD (TA Award)

STEM community connections | No portfolio required

Connects with local STEM community to learn and adopt new tools through effort and persistence.

  • [REQUIRED] Describe team plan with goals for member skill development
  • [REQUIRED] Document steps taken or planned to reach those goals
  • [Encouraged] Examples of connections with engineering/science/tech professionals
  • [Encouraged] Examples of actively engaging with the engineering community

REACH AWARD (TA Award) - NEW!

Recruiting new people into FIRST | No portfolio required

Celebrates teams that introduce and recruit new people into FIRST, sparking others to embrace FIRST culture.

  • [REQUIRED] Describe outreach objectives and how they support FIRST
  • [REQUIRED] Document successful recruitment of NEW teams, coaches, mentors, or volunteers
  • [Encouraged] Be an ambassador for FIRST - make FIRST LOUD!
  • [Encouraged] Creative, evolving approach to outreach materials
  • Jan 10 Robotics Camp at STEM Collegiate supports this award!

SUSTAIN AWARD (TA Award) - NEW!

Long-term team sustainability | No portfolio required

Celebrates teams planning for future members and ensuring the team continues long after current members move on.

  • [REQUIRED] Document plan for: finances, season planning, OR team sustainability
  • [REQUIRED] Show how team tracks progress towards their plan(s)
  • [Encouraged] Clear team roles for all members + leadership development process
  • [Encouraged] Document how team manages constraints and/or risks

INNOVATE AWARD (MCI Award)

Sponsored by RTX | No portfolio required

Celebrates creativity and inventiveness - innovative design solution to game components.

  • [REQUIRED] Describe/display engineering content showing HOW you arrived at your design
  • [REQUIRED] Robot or mechanism is creative and/or unique in design
  • [REQUIRED] Innovative element is stable, robust, contributes to game objectives MOST of the time
  • [Encouraged] Discuss how you mitigated design risks

DESIGN AWARD (MCI Award)

Industrial design excellence | No portfolio required

Balance of form, function, and aesthetics. Robot efficiently designed and effectively addresses game challenge.

  • [REQUIRED] Robot is elegant, efficient (simple), and practical to maintain
  • [REQUIRED] Entire machine design (not just one component) is worthy of recognition
  • [Encouraged] Robot distinguishes itself by aesthetic and functional design
  • [Encouraged] Well-considered design basis (inspiration, function)
  • [Encouraged] Design consistent with team's game plan/strategy

CONTROL AWARD (MCI Award)

Sensors & software | PORTFOLIO REQUIRED

Uses sensors and software to increase robot functionality. Solutions for autonomous or teleop.

  • [REQUIRED] PORTFOLIO with: hardware/software components on robot
  • [REQUIRED] PORTFOLIO with: which challenges each component solves
  • [REQUIRED] PORTFOLIO with: how each component/system works
  • [REQUIRED] Use hardware/software to improve robot using external feedback
  • [Encouraged] Control solution works consistently during most matches
  • [Encouraged] Document lessons learned in developing control solutions

✅ Master Competition Checklist

📄 Portfolio Requirements (CRITICAL)

Your portfolio is reviewed for multiple awards. Maximum 15 pages + cover page.

  • Cover page with team number and nameREQUIRED
  • Team section: member bios with photos, roles, interests
  • Engineering section: design process, CAD, sketches, iterations
  • Software section: code screenshots with descriptions
  • Outreach section: community activities with photos
  • Game strategy section: explain your approach
  • Summary page attached to cover for judgesREQUIRED
  • Under 15 pages (cover not counted)REQUIRED
  • File size under 15MB if digital

🤖 Robot Inspection Checklist

Your robot must pass inspection before competing.

  • Robot meets size requirements (18" x 18" x 18" starting config)REQUIRED
  • Weight within limits (42 lbs max)REQUIRED
  • All parts are competition-legal
  • Wiring is neat, secured, and follows rules
  • Team number clearly visible on two sidesREQUIRED
  • Self-inspect using official checklist before event
  • Student present during inspectionREQUIRED

🎤 Judging Presentation Checklist

You'll have 10 minutes to present to judges.

  • Prepare 5-7 minute presentation (leave time for questions)
  • ALL students should speak (judges notice this)IMPORTANT
  • Practice presentation multiple times as a team
  • Bring robot for "show and tell" if possible
  • Prepare answers to common judge questions
  • Know your portfolio well - judges will ask about it
  • Dress professionally (team shirts if you have them)

📦 Competition Day Packing List

  • Robot and robot cart
  • Printed portfolio (bring extras)
  • Spare parts and tools
  • Laptop with code (charged!)
  • Phone chargers for controllers
  • Safety glasses for pit work
  • Snacks and water
  • Team banner/display for pit area

📝 Engineering Notebook Guide

📚 Engineering Notebook Best Practices

Your engineering notebook/portfolio is CRITICAL for judging. Document everything from Day 1!

What to Include

  • Date and time for each entry
  • Who was present at each meeting
  • Goals for each session
  • Design sketches and CAD drawings
  • Code screenshots with explanations
  • Test results (successes AND failures)
  • What you learned and next steps
  • Photos of build progress
  • Outreach activities documentation

Pro Tips

  • Document failures! Judges love seeing growth
  • Use two-column format: Task | Reflections
  • Include enough detail to recreate your robot
  • Cite sources for any borrowed ideas
  • Add page flags for important sections
  • Create summary page highlighting key pages
  • Real-time documentation is easier than backfilling
  • Photos and diagrams speak louder than words

💡 Pro Tips for Rookie Teams

🧠 Strategy Tips

  • Read the game manual at least 3 times - rules are complex
  • Watch match videos from other regions on YouTube
  • Start with a simple robot that does 1-2 things well
  • Practice autonomous - those points are guaranteed
  • Scout other teams at competition to find good alliance partners

🏆 Awards Tips

  • Awards are 50% of advancement - don't ignore them!
  • Your portfolio matters more than you think
  • Every student should speak during judging presentation
  • Document your journey, including failures
  • Community outreach can set you apart as a rookie

🤝 Team Culture Tips

  • Assign clear roles to each team member
  • Have a dedicated notebook person at every meeting
  • Celebrate small wins to build momentum
  • Ask veteran teams for help - they want to mentor!
  • Gracious Professionalism is noticed by everyone

🛠 Competition Day Tips

  • Arrive at least 30 minutes early
  • Check in and get inspection done first thing
  • Have backup parts and batteries ready
  • Stay calm - problems happen to everyone
  • Cheer for other teams and make friends!

Remember: You're Already Winners!

As a first-year team, everything you accomplish is a learning experience. Look at the insane progress you've made already from Meet 1 to Meet 2! Judges love seeing rookie teams with enthusiasm and strong documentation. Your journey matters more than a perfect robot. Focus on learning, documenting your process, and having fun!
