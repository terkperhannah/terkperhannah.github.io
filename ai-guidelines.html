AI Guidelines for Students

Using AI Responsibly in Our Classroom

Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools like ChatGPT, Grammarly, and Canva AI can support your learning in exciting ways. However, it's important to use them responsibly, ethically, and in a way that helps you grow as a learner. These guidelines explain when and how to use AI appropriately.

When It's Okay to Use AI

  • Brainstorming ideas for writing assignments or projects
  • Getting feedback on grammar or spelling (e.g., Grammarly)
  • Summarizing or simplifying hard-to-understand texts
  • Practicing coding with AI suggestions in IDEs like Visual Studio Code
  • Generating quiz or review questions for personal study

When NOT to Use AI

  • Submitting AI-written content as your own work
  • Using AI to complete math or coding problems without trying first
  • Asking AI to do full assignments or reflections
  • Using AI to look up test answers during assessments

Academic Integrity & Citation

If you use AI to support your learning, you must be transparent about how. This includes noting which tool you used, what you asked it, and how it helped you.

Example citation (APA 7): ChatGPT. (2024, April 12). Response to “Give three pros of using AI in education.” OpenAI. https://chat.openai.com/

We value your original voice — AI should support your thinking, not replace it.
