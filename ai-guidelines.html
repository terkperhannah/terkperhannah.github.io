ISTE Standards for Students

Explore the 7 ISTE Standards for Students through practical classroom applications and digital tools. Each standard includes real-world examples and recommended resources to enhance your learning experience.

1. Empowered Learner

Student-Friendly Explanation: Take ownership of your learning journey by setting personal goals, monitoring your progress with digital tools, and seeking feedback.

Classroom Connection: Track your math skill development using Khan Academy's progress dashboard and personalized practice recommendations.

Visual: Khan Academy progress tracking. Explore their learning platform.

2. Digital Citizen

Student-Friendly Explanation: Practice safe, ethical, and responsible use of digital resources in all online interactions.

Classroom Connection: Properly attribute Creative Commons licensed images in your science presentations using Wikimedia Commons.

Example of proper image attribution. Learn about CC licenses.

3. Knowledge Constructor

Student-Friendly Explanation: Curate and synthesize information from digital sources to create meaningful projects.

Classroom Connection: Develop a climate change presentation using trusted sources and PowerPoint's research tools.

Presentation creation in PowerPoint. Microsoft 365 tools for education.

4. Innovative Designer

Student-Friendly Explanation: Develop creative solutions to real-world problems through iterative design processes.

Classroom Connection: Prototype an educational video game about ecosystems using MakeCode Arcade.

Game design with MakeCode. Explore coding projects.

5. Computational Thinker

Student-Friendly Explanation: Solve complex problems by breaking them into logical steps supported by technology.

Classroom Connection: Program a micro:bit sensor system to monitor classroom air quality.

Physical computing with micro:bit. Start coding.

6. Creative Communicator

Student-Friendly Explanation: Express ideas effectively using multimedia tools tailored to your audience.

Classroom Connection: Create an interactive Canva infographic to explain historical events.

Digital design with Canva. Explore templates.

7. Global Collaborator

Student-Friendly Explanation: Collaborate across distances using digital tools to address worldwide challenges.

Classroom Connection: Co-author a multicultural cookbook with international peers using Teams shared documents.

Virtual teamwork in Teams. Learn about collaboration.
