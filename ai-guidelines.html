AI Guidelines for Students

Using AI Responsibly in Our Classroom

Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools like ChatGPT, Grammarly, and Canva AI can be powerful learning assistants when used properly. This guide helps you use AI ethically while developing your own skills and creativity.

Approved AI Uses

  • Brainstorming project ideas or writing topics
  • Improving grammar/spelling with tools like Grammarly
  • Simplifying complex texts for better understanding
  • Getting coding suggestions in Visual Studio Code
  • Creating practice questions for self-study

Restricted AI Uses

  • Submitting AI-generated content as your own work
  • Solving math/coding problems without attempting first
  • Completing entire assignments using AI
  • Accessing test answers during assessments

Academic Integrity & Citation

When using AI tools, always disclose:

Example Citation (APA 7):
ChatGPT. (2024, April 12). Response to "Three educational benefits of AI". OpenAI. https://chat.openai.com/

Remember: AI should enhance your learning, not replace your original thinking!
